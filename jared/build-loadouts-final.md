# Build Loadouts — Re-Evaluated (Monument of Triumph 9.7.0)

> Full Opus re-evaluation against the **authoritative artifact data** (datamine perks+tiers, video-confirmed; PlayerAuctions guide descriptions). Every build is **tier-legal**: 2 slots Tier-1-only + 3 slots T1/2 + 2 slots T1/2/3 → **≥2 Tier-1, ≤2 Tier-3**. Weapons split into **Own** (best from your vault) and **Farm** (objective best-in-slot upgrades). Anti-champion is intrinsic to frames now — no anti-champ perks.

## Stormwrath - Geomag Chaos Reach / Ionic Sentry Bolt Charge (Arc Stormcaller, boss-DPS Super-spam + Arc add-clear)
**Artifact: Tablet of Ruin** — Tablet of Ruin is the only artifact in the pool whose perk web is built around this loop's three pillars: Bolt Charge, Amplified, and Arc Super energy. Dielectric -> Flashover is a self-contained Bolt Charge -> Amplified -> bolt-damage engine; Photonic Flare/Defibrillating Blast layer Blind and refill Bolt Charge; Elemental Supercharger and Limit Break directly feed and buff Chaos Reach. No other artifact touches Bolt Charge at all - Hunter's Journal and NPA have scattered Arc perks (Shock and Awe, Bad Amplitude, Galvanic Armor) but no Bolt Charge or Arc-Super-energy column, so they cannot drive a Geomag Super-spam loop. This is mechanical fit, not element matching: the build literally cannot exist without Dielectric/Flashover/Elemental Supercharger, which only Tablet provides.

**Perks (7):**

| Perk | Tier | Why |
|---|:--:|---|
| Dielectric | T1 | Core engine: defeating Arc-debuffed (jolted/blinded) targets grants Bolt Charge and rapid kills spawn an Orb + heal. Your Ionic Sentry blind + Jolting Feedback jolt mean nearly every add kill becomes Bolt Charge stacks and sustain - the input that drives Flashover. |
| Flashover | T2 | Reaching max Bolt Charge makes you Amplified and boosts lightning-bolt damage. With Dielectric feeding stacks constantly this gives near-permanent Amplified (DR + Spark of Amplitude orbs) and is the prerequisite Arc buff that switches on Limit Break during Chaos Reach. |
| Photonic Flare | T1 | Defeating Exhausted/Severed targets with Arc releases a blinding burst; Exhausted also cuts enemy outgoing damage. Layers Blind onto your jolt chains for more Dielectric procs and Electrostatic Mind triggers, plus real GM defensive value while you stand still channeling the beam. |
| Elemental Siphon | T1 | Rapid Arc (Super-matching) or Kinetic kills create Ionic Traces that grant Arc Super energy on pickup. This is the universal refill that, stacked with Geomag's +7% per Trace, keeps Chaos Reach coming back fast - the backbone of a Super-spam build. |
| Hold the Line | T1 | While surrounded, Machine Guns/Glaives get reload+stability and final blows heal. Covers the between-Super Heavy DPS window with sustain and survivability when add density (which this build creates) is high; fills the 4th T1 to stay tier-legal. |
| Elemental Supercharger | T3 | Defeating Exhausted/Severed targets with Arc weapons grants Super energy directly. Combined with Photonic Flare's exhaust and the Sentry's blind, this turns add-clear into raw Chaos Reach uptime - the single strongest accelerant for the refill loop. |
| Limit Break | T3 | Casting a Super while holding an Arc buff (you are near-permanently Amplified via Flashover) grants increased Super damage. Multiplies on top of Geomag's ~120% extension for the actual boss-DPS phase - a free damage layer on every Chaos Reach cast. |

**Weapons — Own:** **Centrifuse** (energy) — Arc AR exotic and the build's named engine: builds Bolt Charge, makes you Amplified on kills, and generates Ionic Traces. Its add-clear directly feeds Dielectric/Flashover and Elemental Siphon/Supercharger - it occupies the energy slot and is your only exotic. · **Midnight Coup** (kinetic) — Reliable Kinetic add-clear primary. Kinetic final blows satisfy Elemental Siphon (Kinetic counts), creating Arc Ionic Traces for Super energy, so it pulls double duty as a refill source when Arc kills slow down. Leaves the energy slot for Centrifuse and the heavy slot for boss DPS. · **Apex Predator** (power) — Top owned boss-DPS rocket (Bait and Switch + Vorpal). Provides the burst Heavy window between Chaos Reach casts where this build's single-target damage otherwise sags. Stacks behind a Tractor Cannon weaken for a big damage spike alongside the Super.

**Weapons — Farm (best-in-slot upgrades):** **Song of Ir Yut (or any high-stat Arc MG with reload/damage perks)** (power, from Crota's End raid (Arc MG already owned - re-farm for a Hold the Line / sustained-DPS friendly roll)) — Swapping the Solar rocket for an Arc Machine Gun activates Hold the Line (reload+heal while surrounded) AND lets the Heavy window feed Elemental Siphon/Supercharger (Arc = Super-matching) for extra Chaos Reach energy - synergy a Solar rocket cannot provide. Only worth it if you value loop sustain over Apex's raw burst. · **Sweet Sorrow / The Number with Jolting Feedback + Voltshot** (energy, from Sweet Sorrow (Crota's End) / The Number (Nightfall) - both owned; farm for the Jolting Feedback roll) — If you ever drop Centrifuse for a more aggressive jolt engine, a legendary Arc AR with Jolting Feedback is the build's named jolt source and out-jolts Centrifuse for Dielectric/Photonic Flare uptime. Sidegrade, not a strict upgrade while Centrifuse is the exotic.

**Sandbox notes:** Loop: Centrifuse + Jolting Feedback/Ionic Sentry blind enemies -> Dielectric converts those Arc-debuffed kills into Bolt Charge + orbs + heals -> Flashover at max Bolt Charge makes you Amplified and boosts bolts -> Amplified is the Arc buff that arms Limit Break, so when you fire Chaos Reach into a boss you stack Limit Break's Super-damage bonus on top of Geomag's ~120% extension. Refill side: Elemental Siphon turns Arc/Kinetic kills into Ionic Traces (+7% Super each via Geomag) and Elemental Supercharger dumps Super energy on Exhausted/Severed Arc kills, which Photonic Flare's blind-and-exhaust loop keeps generating - that's what makes Chaos Reach near-permanent. KEY EXTERNAL MULTIPLIER: you own Tractor Cannon (Void) - one shot applies 30% damdebuff; firing it right before Chaos Reach + Apex is the biggest single DPS gain available and it stacks with Limit Break/Geomag. Tier-legality: final 7 = 4 T1 (Dielectric, Photonic Flare, Elemental Siphon, Hold the Line) + 1 T2 (Flashover) + 2 T3 (Elemental Supercharger, Limit Break) = legal (>=2 T1, <=2 T3). NOTE the doc's own list ran THREE T3 (Supercharger + Limit Break + Heavy Ordnance Regeneration) which is illegal under the gate; I dropped Heavy Ordnance Regeneration for T1 Hold the Line. Defibrillating Blast (T3, stun Champ -> max Bolt Charge + jolt/heal) is a strong alternative T3 for GMs if you'd rather guarantee Bolt Charge from Champ stuns than run Limit Break, but Limit Break gives more boss-phase damage. Champions need no artifact mods - anti-champ is intrinsic to weapon frames in 9.7.0. All confirmed tiers come from datamine; no tier-0/unconfirmed perk is used, so the T3 cap is solid. Watch the documented Ionic Sentry fragment-interaction bugs (verify Spark of Ions / Electrostatic Mind procs live).

## Busted Buddies — Prismatic Warlock Turret/Buddy Build (Getaway Artist)
**Artifact: Encrypted Data Disk** — This build is fundamentally an Arc-grenade-Jolt + freeze + orb/ability-regen engine, not an element-match exercise. Encrypted Data Disk is the only artifact whose perk density lines up with the actual loop: the thrown Storm Grenade Jolts via Facet of Dominance, and EDD turns those Jolted kills into the build's economy. Dielectric (Arc-debuff kills -> Bolt Charge + Orb + heal), Blinding Jolts (final blows on Jolted targets blind the pack), Elemental Orbs: Arc, and Tazer Tag form a self-reinforcing Arc-jolt cluster, while Counter Energy refunds the least-charged ability (your Grenade) on champ stuns to re-seed Bleak Watcher and the Arc Soul, and Press the Advantage / Reload at Range / Kinetic Synthesis keep your weapons online while three buddies passively clear. Tablet of Ruin (the other Arc-leaning artifact) is real competition but skews toward Bolt-Charge/heavy-weapon and Void/Strand DoT payoffs (Flashover, Defibrillating Blast, Void Flux) and lacks EDD's orb-generation + ability-regen + blind-CC density that a Grenade-stacked sustain GM build actually lives on. EDD wins on interaction count, not color.

**Perks (7):**

| Perk | Tier | Why |
|---|:--:|---|
| Dielectric | T2 | Core economy perk: your tapped Storm Grenades Jolt via Facet of Dominance and the Getaway Arc Soul pours constant Arc damage, so kills on Jolted targets grant Bolt Charge and, on rapid kills, spawn an Orb + heal — feeding the orb-pickup survivability loop (Recuperation/Better Already) and Innervation grenade regen. |
| Counter Energy | T2 | Champions are stunned intrinsically by your weapon frames in 9.7.0, so this fires reliably and refunds your LEAST-charged ability — which on a Grenade-200 build is almost always the Grenade, directly re-seeding Bleak Watcher and the Getaway Arc Soul mid-fight. |
| Blinding Jolts | T2 | Final blows on Jolted combatants blind the surrounding pack. Your Storm Grenade Jolts everything (Facet of Dominance) and the Arc Soul finishes them, so this becomes near-constant AoE blind — a major GM survivability/CC layer, and far more reliable here than Tazer Tag since your equipped melee is Strand (Arcane Needle), not Arc. |
| Press The Advantage | T1 | In add-dense GM rooms you break shields constantly; the stability/handling/reload buff keeps your Arc primary smooth so you stay in the Arc-kill -> orb/jolt loop without fumbling reloads while the buddies work. |
| Reload at Range | T1 | Auto-stacks reload on Snipers/Bows so your single-target boss option (Sniper) stays topped between buddy chip volleys — covers this build's known weak personal DPS during damage phases alongside Needlestorm. |
| Kinetic Synthesis | T1 | Sustained Kinetic damage + rapid kills drop a brick granting Kinetic ammo plus Special/Heavy progress. With Witherhoard in the Kinetic slot this keeps the set-and-forget DoT fed and trickles heavy for the boss phase, propping up the weak weapon-DPS pillar without demanding attention. |
| Elemental Orbs: Arc | T3 | Arc weapon final blows spawn Arc Elemental Orbs that jolt on detonation — these both feed Dielectric/Blinding Jolts (more Jolted targets) and provide the Orb pickups that keep you Amplified and trigger Innervation grenade energy. The single best T3 for this build's Arc-orb economy. |

**Weapons — Own:** **Witherhoard** (Kinetic) — Set-and-forget blight DoT that chips bosses and packs with zero attention cost — perfect for a build whose job is playing the orb/Transcendence economy while three buddies clear. As your only Exotic it leaves Energy/Power free, and its sustained damage + rapid kills trigger Kinetic Synthesis bricks for ammo economy. · **Sweet Sorrow** (Energy) — Legendary Arc Auto Rifle: it is the engine for the artifact's Arc cluster — Arc final blows feed Elemental Orbs: Arc, jolted kills proc Dielectric/Blinding Jolts, and the steady Arc damage keeps you Amplified. A high add-clear Arc primary is exactly what the loop needs in Energy. · **Apex Predator** (Power) — Legendary Solar rocket with Bait-and-Switch/Explosive Light potential — your dedicated damage-phase weapon to layer on top of Needlestorm and the three buddies during boss melt, covering this build's acknowledged low personal burst without spending the Exotic slot.

**Weapons — Farm (best-in-slot upgrades):** **Indebted Kindness** (Energy (Arc special), from Reissued via current Tower/seasonal vendor focusing (Brave/Onslaught-lineage Rocket-Assisted Frame Sidearm)) — A god-roll Arc RASidearm (Voltshot/Demolitionist) outperforms a plain Arc AR for THIS loop: Voltshot Jolts on reload to mass-feed Dielectric + Blinding Jolts + Elemental Orbs: Arc, Demolitionist refunds grenade energy to your 200-Grenade buddy engine, and RASidearm chunk damage gives champion coverage. Beats Sweet Sorrow on jolt-application density and grenade regen. · **VS Chill Inhibitor** (Power, from Vesper's Host dungeon (Strand Heavy Wave-frame GL)) — If your owned roll is subpar, a Bait-and-Switch / Envious Assassin VS Chill Inhibitor is a top current-meta boss heavy that also stuns Unstoppables intrinsically and applies Slow/blind-adjacent control — a cleaner boss-DPS pillar than Apex Predator for sustained phases on a build that needs its heavy to carry damage.

**Sandbox notes:** The whole kit is one Arc-jolt feedback loop sitting on a Grenade-200 buddy chassis. Facet of Dominance Jolts the tapped Storm Grenade -> Dielectric converts Jolted kills into Bolt Charge + Orbs + heals -> Elemental Orbs: Arc spawns jolting orbs that create MORE Jolted targets -> Blinding Jolts blinds the pack off those finals for GM survivability, while Bleak Watcher freeze/slow lets Facet of Courage add +10% buddy damage. Counter Energy closes the loop by refunding your least-charged ability (the Grenade) on intrinsic champion stuns, re-seeding the turret and Arc Soul. Sweet Sorrow's Arc kills + Witherhoard's DoT keep the orb/Amplified economy running so Innervation/Recuperation sustain you hands-free. CORRECTION/FLAG vs the doc: the doc slots Tazer Tag (T3, Arc-MELEE jolt), but the build's equipped melee is Arcane Needle (Strand), so Tazer Tag will NOT reliably proc — I dropped it for Blinding Jolts (T2), which procs off the grenade-driven Jolt the build actually generates. Tier legality of my 7: T1 = Press the Advantage, Reload at Range, Kinetic Synthesis (3, satisfies the >=2 T1 gate); T3 = Elemental Orbs: Arc only (1, within the <=2 T3 cap); remaining T2 = Dielectric, Counter Energy, Blinding Jolts. All datamine-confirmed tiers; no tier-0/unconfirmed perks used. Note Power from Pain (a tempting T1 Devour pick) was rejected because the build has no Weaken source, making its gating unreliable.

## Eternal Embers — Dawn Chorus Infinite Ignition Dawnblade
**Artifact: Queensfoil Censer** — Queensfoil Censer is genuinely best-in-slot here, and not because of "Solar element matching" — it is the only artifact in the pool that contains the full Radiant-to-Scorch-to-Ignition chain this exotic is built around. Dawn Chorus's 9.7.0 rework turns scorch into stronger ignitions, refunds melee on ignition damage, and instantly ignites Daybreak projectiles. The Censer supplies every input that engine consumes: Fever and Chill (Radiant on Solar precision), Kindling Trigger (Radiant makes Solar weapons apply scorch), and Rays of Precision (Radiant precision finals ignite directly). No other artifact (NPA = Void, Hunter's Journal/Slayer = Stasis-leaning, Tablet = Arc/Void/Strand, Encrypted Data Disk = Kinetic/Sword) carries Kindling Trigger + Rays of Precision + Torch, which are the load-bearing multipliers. Torch additionally turns the Radiant uptime into a flat weapon-damage bonus against Scorch-adjacent debuffs, and Heart of the Flame buffs Daybreak for the boss-DPS half of the build's job. It is the correct pick by mechanic interaction, period.

**Perks (7):**

| Perk | Tier | Why |
|---|:--:|---|
| Kindling Trigger | T1 | Core ignition seed: while Radiant, Solar weapons apply scorch to unscorched targets. Every precision hit now stacks scorch across the whole pack, and Ember of Ashes pushes those stacks to ignition threshold faster — this is the input Dawn Chorus amplifies into stronger ignitions. |
| Fever and Chill | T1 | Confirmed-tier Radiant engine — rapid Solar precision hits grant Radiant. I use this instead of Flint Striker because Flint Striker is tier-null (guide-only). Radiant is the on/off switch for Kindling Trigger, Torch, and Rays of Precision, so it must sit on a confirmed slot. |
| Torch | T1 | While Radiant, deal increased weapon damage to combatants affected by Strand/Stasis debuffs. With near-permanent Radiant uptime it is effectively a free sustained weapon-damage layer on top of the scorch loop, and stacks with any Stasis/Strand-debuff splash from teammates or a darkness heavy. |
| Heart of the Flame | T1 | Casting Daybreak grants nearby allies Radiant and increases Super damage per nearby ally (the real datamined effect, not ability refund). This is the boss-DPS half of the build — it directly scales Dawn Chorus's instant-ignite Daybreak and adds team value, replacing the build doc's now-defunct Revitalizing-Blast Weaken plan. |
| Wished into Being | T1 | While Super is nearly charged, ability final blows spawn Orbs of Power. The ignition loop generates constant ability kills, so this drives Orb economy for armor mods (Surges, Recuperation) and feeds super regen for more frequent Daybreaks — a fourth T1 keeps us comfortably above the >=2 T1 gate. |
| Revitalizing Blast | T2 | Stunning a Champion ignites it and clears your shield stun (real effect). In GMs this gives the build free champion ignitions on top of Anti-Champion 2.0 frame stuns and removes the unstoppable/overload shield-stun that otherwise locks you out — strong survivability/utility, occupies the T1-2 slot legally. |
| Rays of Precision | T3 | While Radiant, Solar precision final blows cause ignitions — the single highest-value ignition multiplier in the pool and the literal name of the build's loop. It is Tier 3, so it takes one of our two T3 slots; with only this one T3 used we stay under the <=2 T3 cap with margin. |

**Weapons — Own:** **Witherhoard** (Kinetic) — Kinetic exotic that applies persistent blight DoT, freeing your energy and heavy slots for pure Solar ignition support. Constant chip damage keeps Ember of Empyrean/Searing kills flowing for buff extension and melee refunds even when you are repositioning airborne under Heat Rises. · **Vex Mythoclast** (Energy) — Solar exotic fusion-AR in the energy slot — precision body shots sustain Radiant for Fever and Chill and feed Kindling Trigger scorch on every shot, while Linear-mode burst gives respectable add and minor-boss damage. It is the doc's named pairing and the cleanest single weapon to run the entire Radiant-scorch loop off. · **Dragon's Breath** (Power) — Solar exotic rocket that plants a scorch-spreading pyre — it is an enormous ignition seeder that synergizes 1:1 with Dawn Chorus's stronger-ignition rework and Solar Fulmination-style ignition scaling, and doubles as boss/champion burst during Daybreak windows. Only one exotic equipped: pair it with the non-exotic Vex alternative (Eremite/Marsilion-C) if you keep Vex.

**Weapons — Farm (best-in-slot upgrades):** **The Eremite (Solar Fusion, Adept)** (Energy, from Trials of Osiris / craftable from existing pattern (Brave or seasonal reissue)) — If you want to keep an exotic in the heavy slot, a high-stat Incandescent + Reservoir Burst Solar fusion out-scorches Vex as a non-exotic Radiant/scorch primary-replacement, letting you run Dragon's Breath as your one exotic. Incandescent spreads scorch on kills, compounding Kindling Trigger. · **Solar Wave-Frame GL with Incandescent + Demolitionist (e.g. reissued Marsilion-C / seasonal wave-frame)** (Power, from Seasonal/world drop wave-frame GL, focus at vendor) — Objective add-clear upgrade over a single-target heavy for THIS build: the horizontal wave applies scorch across an entire pack (Incandescent kills spread more), instantly meeting ignition thresholds for chained Dawn Chorus ignitions, while Demolitionist refunds Fusion-grenade energy to re-seed ignitions. The doc explicitly names this as the ideal legendary fit. · **Sunshot (if not the primary)** (Energy, from World/exotic drops, focusing) — Native scorch + ignite-on-kill primary that runs the loop without depending on precision uptime, a safety net against the build's main weakness (Radiant sputtering in target-poor or low-precision situations). Genuine upgrade only when consistent precision is hard to maintain.

**Sandbox notes:** Loop: Solar precision hits -> Fever and Chill Radiant -> Kindling Trigger makes every Solar shot scorch the pack -> Fusion grenade / Incinerator Snap crosses threshold -> Dawn Chorus turns the ignition into a STRONGER ignition that refunds melee, and Rays of Precision adds extra precision-final ignitions. Ember of Empyrean refreshes Restoration/Radiant off Solar kills for near-infinite buff uptime; Ember of Searing refunds melee + spawns Firesprites; Heat Rises gives airborne Cure and melee-on-kill. Torch then taxes the same Radiant uptime into flat weapon damage. For boss phases, Heart of the Flame + Dawn Chorus's instant-igniting Daybreak is the damage window (note: this is Super-damage/ally-Radiant, NOT the Weaken effect the build doc assumed). KEY CORRECTIONS vs the build doc: (1) Heart of the Flame and Revitalizing Blast were mis-described in the doc — datamine shows Heart of the Flame = Super-damage buff and Revitalizing Blast = champion-ignite + shield-stun clear, NOT ability/Weaken effects, so the boss-DPS rationale shifts to Daybreak scaling rather than Weaken setup. (2) Flint Striker is tier-null (guide-only, src 'guide-only (tier?)') — I deliberately swapped it for the datamined Tier-1 Fever and Chill so the Radiant engine sits on a confirmed tier and does not risk the T3 cap accounting. Tier-legality of my 7: five T1 (Kindling Trigger, Fever and Chill, Torch, Heart of the Flame, Wished into Being) + one T2 (Revitalizing Blast) + one T3 (Rays of Precision) = >=2 T1 satisfied, <=2 T3 satisfied with one to spare. Anti-Champion 2.0 means champion stuns come from weapon frames, so the whole artifact stays pointed at Solar damage. Flag: if you insist on Flint Striker for the no-cooldown Radiant feel, it occupies an unconfirmed-tier slot — keep at least two confirmed T1s (Kindling Trigger, Torch) and do not add a second T3.

## Permafrost Bleak Watcher — Shadebinder Frost Armor Engine
**Artifact: Slayer Baron's Apothecary Satchel** — Correct pick, and not for element-matching reasons — it is the only artifact in the pool whose perks physically close the Bleak Watcher freeze-shatter-shard loop. Wind Chill turns every Stasis weapon burst into Frost Armor + shards; Crystalline Converter converts the Glacial Harvest shard flood into on-demand crystal generation off the Penumbral Blast melee; Served Cold converts that same shard flood into class-ability energy (Healing Rift); Hail The Storm and Brain Freeze multiply shatter damage and extend the Slow field. No other artifact (NPA/Void, Tablet/Strand-Arc, Encrypted/Kinetic-Arc, Implement/Solar-Strand) interacts with shards, crystals, freeze, or Frost Armor — they would leave half the kit (Glacial Harvest, Whisper of Rime/Shards/Fissures) doing nothing. This artifact is the engine block this subclass bolts onto.

**Perks (7):**

| Perk | Tier | Why |
|---|:--:|---|
| Wind Chill | T1 | Rapid Stasis weapon hits grant Frost Armor and hits on slowed/frozen targets spawn Stasis Shards. With a Stasis primary always shooting frozen packs from the Bleak Watcher, this is a second shard fountain stacked on Glacial Harvest, and self-sustains Frost Armor independent of pickups — the core Frost-Armor-as-grenade-economy engine of 9.7.0. |
| Crystalline Converter | T1 | Gathering the shards Glacial Harvest + Wind Chill produce builds stacks that make your next Penumbral Blast melee spawn multiple Stasis crystals. Those crystals are free shatter fuel (Whisper of Fissures) and free Coldsnap energy via Whisper of Shards — it manufactures the crystals the doc's Headstone weapon can't always supply. |
| Served Cold | T3 | Picking up a Stasis Shard grants class-ability energy. This is the single highest-value perk for this build: Glacial Harvest spawns shards on every freeze, so you are sweeping shards constantly — converting them directly into Healing Rift uptime, which then feeds Bomber (grenade cooldown) and the new 100-Recovery overshield. Replaces the doc's Conductive Cosmic Crystal, which is dead weight here (see notes). |
| Hail The Storm | T2 | Shattering frozen targets and crystals deals increased damage AND releases slowing ice shards that damage nearby foes. This is the build's only real add-clear amplifier — every shatter becomes chained AoE plus more Slow, turning the freeze loop into actual kills, not just CC. |
| Brain Freeze | T3 | Frozen combatants emit chilling fog that Slows everything nearby. With permanent freeze uptime from the turret + Coldsnap, this passively blankets the whole room in Slow with zero extra input — doubling the lockdown radius and feeding Wind Chill's 'hits on slowed targets make shards' clause. |
| Frost Renewal | T2 | While you have Frost Armor (which you always do here), taking critical damage releases a freeze burst and grants Frost Armor to you and allies. A free panic-freeze + survivability layer that triggers exactly when a GM is about to kill you, on top of Whisper of Chains and Healing Rift. |
| Total Carnage | T1 | Finishing a powerful combatant grants damage resistance and heals — a reliable T1 survivability backstop for the dense GM/Master add waves this control build deliberately stands inside, and a clean second Tier-1 to satisfy the gate alongside Wind Chill and Crystalline Converter. |

**Weapons — Own:** **Rufus's Fury (Adept) — Stasis Auto Rifle w/ Headstone** (Kinetic) — Goes in the Kinetic slot as a darkness weapon. Headstone spawns a Stasis crystal on precision kills, continuously feeding the shatter loop (Hail The Storm / Whisper of Fissures) and triggering Wind Chill's shard + Frost Armor clause on every frozen pack. Best-in-vault Stasis primary you own for this build's crystal economy; Adept lets you slot a Stasis Surge-friendly setup. · **Conditional Finality (Exotic, Strand/Stasis Shotgun)** (Energy) — Your one Exotic, in the Energy slot. The Stasis barrel freezes on the burst — an instant single-target/Champion freeze-and-shatter you can fire on demand to lock an elite, then shatter for burst. Pairs perfectly with Hail The Storm and gives you a freeze source that doesn't depend on grenade cooldown. · **Pro Memoria — Stasis Machine Gun** (Power) — Stasis Heavy keeps your whole loadout shard/Stasis-Surge aligned and lets a high-impact/aggressive frame intrinsically stun Unstoppables under Anti-Champion 2.0. It is your sustained boss/major DPS where shatter falls off, while still benefiting from Conductive-free Stasis weapon surges and Wind Chill Frost Armor.

**Weapons — Farm (best-in-slot upgrades):** **Cold Comfort (Strand Rocket Launcher) or a Stasis-aligned Heavy GL like VS Chill Inhibitor** (Power, from Cold Comfort: Salvation's Edge raid (final encounter). VS Chill Inhibitor: Vesper's Host activity.) — This build's stated weakness is boss DPS. A Bait-and-Switch/Explosive Light rocket or an Envious/Bait Heavy GL out-damages Pro Memoria by a wide margin in damage phases, while VS Chill Inhibitor's Stasis frame stays on-theme and applies Slow. Only worth farming if you lack a top-tier Heavy DPS option you already trust. · **A god-roll Stasis primary with Headstone + Demolitionist (e.g. Nightshade or a Brya/Iterative-tier Stasis SMG)** (Kinetic, from Current seasonal/Onslaught loot pools; craftable Stasis frames at the Enclave where available.) — If your Rufus's Fury roll lacks Headstone, farm one that has Headstone (crystal spawning) plus Demolitionist (grenade energy on kill) to directly accelerate Bleak Watcher uptime in the 125%-cap cooldown sandbox — a real upgrade only if your owned Stasis primary doesn't already have Headstone.

**Sandbox notes:** Loop: Healing Rift (Bomber) → hold-grenade Bleak Watcher on the dense lane → Coldsnap (Osmiomancy 2nd charge + refund) on a second pack → frozen targets spawn shards (Glacial Harvest) AND emit Slow fog (Brain Freeze) → shoot frozen packs with Rufus's Fury: Wind Chill makes more shards + Frost Armor, Headstone seeds crystals → shatter for AoE (Hail The Storm + Whisper of Fissures) → sweep shards: each one refreshes Frost Armor (Whisper of Rime, the grenade-energy engine) AND grants Healing Rift energy (Served Cold) → Whisper of Shards refunds grenade on crystal shatter, closing the turret loop. Crystalline Converter banks those shards into a multi-crystal Penumbral Blast for burst. Survivability triple-stacks: Frost Armor DR + Whisper of Chains near crystals + Frost Renewal/Total Carnage panic layers. Anti-Champion 2.0 is intrinsic now — bring matching weapon frames (your Stasis Heavy stuns Unstops), no artifact mod needed, which is exactly why none of the artifact's champion-mod perks made the cut. FLAG — the build doc's Conductive Cosmic Crystal (T3) is a trap: it only buffs Arc and Void ABILITIES against Stasis-debuffed targets, but this subclass casts zero Arc/Void abilities (Coldsnap, Bleak Watcher, Penumbral Blast, Winter's Wrath are all Stasis), so it would do literally nothing — I swapped it for Served Cold. TIER NOTE: I used two Tier-3 perks (Served Cold, Brain Freeze) which is the legal maximum; both are confirmed datamine tier 3, not guide-only/unconfirmed. Tier gate satisfied: 3× T1 (Wind Chill, Crystalline Converter, Total Carnage), 2× T2 (Hail The Storm, Frost Renewal), 2× T3 — ≥2 T1 and ≤2 T3.

## Threadling Nest Broodweaver (Necrotic Swarm) — Strand Warlock, Necrotic Grip, GM all-round add-clear
**Artifact: Implement of Curiosity** — It is the only artifact that carries the build's entire Threadling/Tangle/Unravel toolkit in one place, and the only one with a Threadling-DAMAGE scaler. The rocket-perk chain only fires on Loadout B (Apex Predator): Blast Radius banks Armor Charge during add-clear -> Argent Ordnance spends it for the damage phase -> Perpetual Destruction refunds ammo/reload (stacking with Lunafaction) -> Anti-Champion Nosecone adds flat Champion damage. On Loadout A (Tractor Cannon), Argent Ordnance/Nosecone/Perpetual Destruction/Blast Radius go DARK — accept that, because on that loadout your contribution is the 30% weaken + the Well itself, not personal rocket DPS. Radiant uptime is the connective tissue: Fever and Chill (on-demand) + Kindling Trigger (Scorch while radiant) feed Ember of Empyrean/Benevolence, which refills Rift/grenade/Super off adds — the build's answer to the 60% boss-Super nerf, reinforced by Bad Juju's kill-fed Super on Loadout B. TIER-UNCERTAIN FLAGS: I used zero tier-null perks, so the T3 cap is safe. Perk set is tier-legal: 4x T1 (Heart of the Flame, Blast Radius, Fever and Chill, Kindling Trigger) satisfies the >=2 T1 floor, 2x T2 (Anti-Champion Nosecone, Perpetual Destruction), 1x T3 (Argent Ordnance) is under the <=2 T3 cap. Note: the build doc's quoted Queensfoil perk descriptions (e.g. it lists Revitalizing Blast as 'rocket/GL final blows grant healing') differ from the authoritative pool, where Revitalizing Blast is actually 'Stunning a Champion causes it to ignite and clears shield stun' — a Champion-utility perk, not a heal, so I dropped it in favor of the real rocket-DPS perks. Torch (T1) was a near-miss cut: it only buffs damage vs Strand/Stasis-debuffed targets, and your debuff is Void (Tractor) — so it's off-element for this loadout and correctly benched despite being Solar-flavored.
