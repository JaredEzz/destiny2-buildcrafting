## The core mechanic (why you were right and the v1 doc was wrong)
Rain of Fire's intrinsic is **Soaring Fusilier**, verified verbatim from the manifest:

> *"**Air dodge** reloads all of your weapons and improves the airborne effectiveness of Fusion Rifles and Linear Fusion Rifles; final blows with these weapons make you **radiant**."*

It says **"Air dodge,"** not "Icarus Dash." **Weavewalk's** activation is literally *"Dodge while airborne to enter the weave"* (Bungie's own description) — it is an air dodge. Therefore, with **Rain of Fire boots equipped on a Strand subclass running Weavewalk**:
- Every Weavewalk dodge **reloads all three weapons from reserves** (the Icarus-Dash reload, recreated on Strand).
- **Fusion / Linear Fusion final blows make you Radiant** — Rain of Fire's Radiant is granted by the *exotic*, not the subclass, so it fires on Strand exactly as it did on Solar. **Radiant is back.**
- Weavewalk *also* grants **airborne damage resistance** while weaving **and generates Threadlings over time** — so the same aspect that gives the reload also gives survivability and the Strand economy.

Net: switching the exotic from Mataiodoxia to Rain of Fire recovers **both** things the v1 doc listed as unfixable losses (the reload *and* Radiant), and Weavewalk's threadlings + a Strand fusion rifle supply the Tangle economy the v1 doc was missing. This build is strictly better than the Mataiodoxia version.

You asked for a Strand fusion rifle — here two of the three are Strand, feeding the Tangle/Threadling economy, while Vex stays as the irreplaceable primary-ammo engine. All three are fusions, so all three proc Rain of Fire's reload + Radiant. Legal Kinetic/Energy/Power trio with exactly one exotic (Vex).
- **Vex Mythoclast** (exotic, **Energy** slot, primary ammo) — the infinite-ammo fusion workhorse; spray freely, ramps to linear on kills, and every kill can top up Radiant via Rain of Fire. It's the only primary-ammo fusion in the game, which is why it stays even though it's Solar.
- **Resounding** (**Kinetic** slot, **Strand** fusion, special) — *the Strand fusion rifle.* Rolled **Hatchling** (final blows spawn a Threadling) + One for All. This is the Tangle/Threadling engine the v1 build lacked: Hatchling Threadlings make Strand kills → Tangles, which lights up the whole Strand artifact column.

### Aspects
1. **Weavewalk** *(mandatory — this is the build)* — the air dodge that procs Rain of Fire's reload + Radiant, plus airborne DR while weaving, plus passive Threadling generation.
2. **Weaver's Call** — casting Rift weaves and releases Threadlings; stacks with Weavewalk's generation for a steady swarm and a constant Strand-kill → Tangle supply. *(Swap to **The Wanderer** if you want thrown-Tangle suspend CC instead of more Threadlings.)*

### Fragments
- **Thread of Ascent** — grenade use reloads your equipped weapon and boosts airborne effectiveness. **Directly stacks with the dodge reload** and buffs airborne fusion accuracy — the single most on-theme fragment for this build.
- **Thread of Generation** — weapon damage → grenade energy; three fusions firing keeps Threadling Grenade (and Thread of Ascent's reload) coming back fast.
- **Thread of Warding** — Orb pickup → Woven Mail; survivability layer that pairs with Weavewalk's DR.
- **Thread of Evolution** — Threadlings deal more damage and travel farther; scales the Weavewalk / Weaver's Call swarm.

## Gameplay Loop
1. **Get airborne → Weavewalk dodge** = all three fusions reload (Soaring Fusilier) + you're now DR'd in the weave + Threadlings spawning.
2. **Spray Vex / Resounding** — fusion kills grant **Radiant** (Rain of Fire); Resounding's Hatchling drops Threadlings → Tangles.
3. **Cast Healing Rift** — Weaver's Call releases a Threadling volley; heals; re-ups the swarm.
4. **Air-dodge again** to reload and refresh the weave — this is the core rhythm; you almost never touch a manual reload.
5. Boss phase: pre-stack **Particle Reconstruction** with Vex (free primary ammo), then dump **Scintillation** Bait-and-Switch bursts into the debuff, air-dodging between mags for instant reloads. Radiant + Surge + the PR debuff = real DPS.

## Strengths
Recovers the entire Rain of Fire fantasy on Strand: dodge-reload, permanent Radiant off fusion kills, aerial DPS — **plus** a Threadling/Tangle add-clear economy and airborne damage resistance that the Solar original never had. Tablet of Ruin runs near-zero dead weight here.

## Weaknesses (honest)
1. **Aerial exposure** — you're airborne to keep the loop going; Weavewalk's DR and Woven Mail offset it but a stray sniper in a GM still hurts. Same core risk as the Solar Rain of Fire build.
2. **Mixed elements dilute Surge** — Vex is Solar, the DPS guns are Strand; you can only Surge one element (take Strand). Minor DPS loss vs. a mono-element loadout.
3. **Radiant depends on landing fusion kills** — in a boss-only room with no adds, you must tag something with a fusion to refresh Radiant; usually trivial (Vex on the boss) but worth knowing.
