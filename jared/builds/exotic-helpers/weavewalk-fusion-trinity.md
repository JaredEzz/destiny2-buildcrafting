## The core mechanic (why you were right and the v1 doc was wrong)
Rain of Fire's intrinsic is **Soaring Fusilier**, verified verbatim from the manifest:

> *"**Air dodge** reloads all of your weapons and improves the airborne effectiveness of Fusion Rifles and Linear Fusion Rifles; final blows with these weapons make you **radiant**."*

It says **"Air dodge,"** not "Icarus Dash." **Weavewalk's** activation is literally *"Dodge while airborne to enter the weave"* (Bungie's own description) — it is an air dodge. Therefore, with **Rain of Fire boots equipped on a Strand subclass running Weavewalk**:
- Every Weavewalk dodge **reloads all three weapons from reserves** (the Icarus-Dash reload, recreated on Strand).
- **Fusion / Linear Fusion final blows make you Radiant** — Rain of Fire's Radiant is granted by the *exotic*, not the subclass, so it fires on Strand exactly as it did on Solar. **Radiant is back.**
- Weavewalk *also* grants **airborne damage resistance** while weaving **and generates Threadlings over time** — so the same aspect that gives the reload also gives survivability and the Strand economy.

Net: switching the exotic from Mataiodoxia to Rain of Fire recovers **both** things the v1 doc listed as unfixable losses (the reload *and* Radiant), and Weavewalk's threadlings + a Strand fusion rifle supply the Tangle economy the v1 doc was missing. This build is strictly better than the Mataiodoxia version.

## Weapons — Strand Fusion Trinity
You asked for a Strand fusion rifle — here two of the three are Strand, feeding the Tangle/Threadling economy, while Vex stays as the irreplaceable primary-ammo engine. All three are fusions, so all three proc Rain of Fire's reload + Radiant. Legal Kinetic/Energy/Power trio with exactly one exotic (Vex).
- **Vex Mythoclast** (exotic, **Energy** slot, primary ammo) — the infinite-ammo fusion workhorse; spray freely, ramps to linear on kills, and every kill can top up Radiant via Rain of Fire. It's the only primary-ammo fusion in the game, which is why it stays even though it's Solar.
- **Resounding** (**Kinetic** slot, **Strand** fusion, special) — *the Strand fusion rifle.* Rolled **Hatchling** (final blows spawn a Threadling) + One for All. This is the Tangle/Threadling engine the v1 build lacked: Hatchling Threadlings make Strand kills → Tangles, which lights up the whole Strand artifact column.
- **Scintillation (Adept)** (**Power**, **Strand** Linear Fusion, heavy) — rolled **Bait and Switch** (you own a favorited B&S copy). Strand LFR for boss DPS into the Particle Reconstruction debuff — replaces Cataclysmic and, being Strand, also feeds the Tangle economy instead of sitting outside it. *(This fixes the v1 doc's honest caveat that the owned Cataclysmic didn't actually roll Bait and Switch — Scintillation does.)*

**Verb backups** (verbs stun too): Arcane Needle → **Unravel → Barrier**; Suspend (if you run The Wanderer/Shackle) → **Unstoppable**.

**The gap is Overload** — none of the three frames nor your Strand verbs (Unravel=Barrier, Ignition/Suspend=Unstoppable) cover it. Two ways to patch it per activity:
- **Chill Clip Deliverance** (owned) in the Kinetic slot instead of Resounding — Chill Clip **Slow stuns Overload** *and* its **Freeze stuns Unstoppable**, so one gun covers Overload + Unstoppable (you keep Vex/Scintillation Barrier). Cost: you lose Resounding's Hatchling threadling engine.
- **Scatter Signal** (owned, Rapid-Fire Strand fusion) → **Overload** frame, if you'd rather keep a Strand special than run Chill Clip.

So realistically it's "cover two champion types by default (Barrier + Unstoppable), swap the special to reach Overload when an activity demands it."

## Orb generation — low by design, and that's fine
**This build barely makes its own orbs, on purpose.** Siphon mods only proc on *weapon* final blows, but your rapid kills come from **threadlings, Tangles, and Needlestorm** — all *ability* kills that don't trigger a Siphon. Your Strand special/heavy are parked for champions and yellow-bars, not spraying trash, so a Strand (Harmonic) Siphon would sit dead. The only rapid *weapon* killer is **Vex**, which is Solar — so if you want any self-orbs at all, the correct mod is **Solar Siphon** (helmet), and even that's a trickle since threadlings do most of the killing.

So the ability economy deliberately runs on **elemental pickups**, not orbs: the artifact's **Elemental Siphon** turns Vex + Strand-fusion kills into elemental pickups → **Elemental Charge** converts those to Armor Charge → **Time Dilation** holds it → **Strand Weapon Surge** spends it on the DPS guns. Plus **Thread of Generation** for grenade energy. None of that needs an orb.

Where orbs *do* matter — **Thread of Warding → Woven Mail** (and therefore Thread of Transmutation's Tangles) — **you lean on your fireteam's orbs.** In a coordinated team you'll have Woven Mail up plenty off teammates' orb spam; **solo, expect Woven Mail/Transmutation uptime to be spotty** and treat Weavewalk's own DR as your primary airborne survivability instead.

## Gameplay Loop
1. **Get airborne → Weavewalk dodge** = all three fusions reload (Soaring Fusilier) + you're now DR'd in the weave + Threadlings spawning.
2. **Spray Vex / Resounding** — fusion kills grant **Radiant** (Rain of Fire); Resounding's Hatchling drops Threadlings → Tangles.
3. **Cast Healing Rift** — Weaver's Call releases a Threadling volley; heals; re-ups the swarm.
4. **Air-dodge again** to reload and refresh the weave — this is the core rhythm; you almost never touch a manual reload.
5. Boss phase: pre-stack **Particle Reconstruction** with Vex (free primary ammo), then dump **Scintillation** Bait-and-Switch bursts into the debuff, air-dodging between mags for instant reloads. Radiant + Surge + the PR debuff = real DPS.

## Strengths
Recovers the entire Rain of Fire fantasy on Strand: dodge-reload, permanent Radiant off fusion kills, aerial DPS — **plus** a Threadling/Tangle add-clear economy and airborne damage resistance that the Solar original never had. Tablet of Ruin runs near-zero dead weight here.

## Weaknesses (honest)
1. **Low self-orb generation is baked in** — threadling/Tangle/super kills don't proc Siphon and the Strand guns aren't spraying trash, so you make very few orbs yourself. The build is built around that (elemental pickups drive the economy instead), but it means **Woven Mail / Thread of Transmutation lean on fireteam-generated orbs** — noticeably weaker solo. Bring the loadout to a team, not a lonely GM run, for full value.
2. **No Overload coverage by default** — Barrier (Vex/Scintillation) + Unstoppable (Resounding) only; swap to Chill Clip Deliverance or Scatter Signal for Overload (see Champions).
3. **Aerial exposure** — you're airborne to keep the loop going; Weavewalk's DR offsets it but a stray sniper in a GM still hurts. Same core risk as the Solar Rain of Fire build.
4. **Radiant depends on landing fusion kills** — in a boss-only room with no adds, you must tag something with a fusion to refresh Radiant; usually trivial (Vex on the boss) but worth knowing.
