## Exotic — Boots of the Assembler
Blessing of Order: while you stand in a Rift (or Well of Radiance), it generates Noble Seekers that fly out to allies who are NOT inside a rift. In a Healing Rift / Well they heal those allies; in an Empowering Rift they grant a damage buff to you and the ally. Each Seeker that reaches an ally briefly extends your rift's duration while you remain in it, so an actively-helping rift can persist far beyond its base timer. It is also the only exotic that synergizes with Lumina's Noble Rounds — the two together blanket the whole fireteam in heals. In Update 9.7.0 all Armor 3.0 exotics now drop at Tier 5 stats with all tuning mods unlocked, so you can chase a perfect-stat pair without re-grinding.

## Abilities
- **Super:** Well of Radiance (primary — drop the Well, plant the Banner; the safety-net team buff). Song of Flame is the alt super if your fireteam already has a dedicated Well-holder and you want more uptime/add-clear, but Well is the support default.
- **Class Ability:** Healing Rift (this is the engine — Boots of the Assembler turns it into Noble Seekers that fly out and heal allies who are NOT standing in your rift)
- **Grenade:** Healing Grenade (doubled and given Restoration by Touch of Flame; the single best ability for keeping a fireteam alive and the cleanest Ember of Benevolence trigger)
- **Melee:** Incinerator Snap (ranged, applies Scorch to feed Firesprites and ignitions) — Celestial Fire is a fine alternative if you prefer a longer-range powered hit for proccing Ember of Torches' Radiant

### Aspects
- Touch of Flame (doubles Healing Grenade output and grants Restoration to allies it hits — turns one grenade into a team-wide heal)
- Heat Rises (consume your grenade for sustained aerial control plus Cure on consume, and melee energy from airborne final blows; in 9.7.0 it now also reduces enemy accuracy in PvE, adding a passive damage-mitigation layer to your support kit). Hellion is the alt aspect if you want an autonomous Scorch/ignition source instead of aerial play.

### Fragments
- Ember of Benevolence (massively boosted grenade/melee/class regen for ~10s after applying Cure, Restoration, or Radiant to an ally — the keystone fragment, near-permanently active in a support build)
- Ember of Torches (powered melee hits grant Radiant to you and nearby allies — pairs with Incinerator Snap/Celestial Fire)
- Ember of Mercy (grant Restoration to nearby allies when you pick up a Firesprite or revive a teammate — extra ally sustain)
- Ember of Searing (defeating Scorched targets spawns a Firesprite and grants melee energy — fuels Mercy and the Firesprite economy)

## Weapons
- **Solar Wave-Frame GL (Demolitionist + Incandescent)** — your area-denial GL: Incandescent spreads Scorch (feeding ignitions/Firesprites) while Demolitionist refunds grenade energy. Excellent legendary fit for this build's Solar scorch/grenade economy.
- Lumina (Exotic Kinetic hand cannon) — MANDATORY pairing. Noble Rounds: kills spawn remnants that, when collected, let your next shot heal + damage-buff an ally. It is the only weapon that interacts with Boots of the Assembler and doubles your healing throughput.
- An Incandescent Solar primary as a secondary slot option for self-Scorch/ignition uptime (e.g., Solar Calus Mini-Tool / Incandescent SMG) to keep Firesprites and Ember procs flowing — note Lumina occupies your one exotic weapon slot, so this pick must be legendary
- Heavy: a strong boss-DPS option for the windows when the team isn't taking damage — e.g., a Solar rocket (Hothead/Apex) or a Heavy GL; pick by frame for the new intrinsic anti-champion (Aggressive/Rocket frames = Unstoppable)

## Stat Priority
Class > Health > Grenade > Weapons > Super > Melee. Push Class past 100 first (faster rift regen + the new 101–200 overshield-on-cast layer, and Class >100 buffs several abilities); get Health to 100 (Orb HP + flinch resist) and ideally past 100 for the shield-capacity/recharge layer; ~70 Grenade so Healing Grenade comes back fast for constant Benevolence procs; Weapons mid for ammo economy; Super only as needed since Well is cheap to refund; Melee is the dump stat (your melee is a utility/Scorch tool, not damage).

## Artifact — Queensfoil Censer *(re-evaluated)*

Of the 7 artifacts, only Queensfoil Censer reinforces the actual job of this build: a Well of Radiance / Lumina team-sustain anchor that also clears Scorch/ignition adds. The decisive interaction is Heart of the Flame, whose REAL effect (per the authoritative pool, not the build doc's stale text) is 'Casting your Solar Super grants nearby allies Radiant and increases your Super damage for each nearby ally' — that is a Well-of-Radiance support perk almost written for this loadout, turning every Well cast into a team Radiant blanket on top of the Well's own buff. Around it the artifact stacks self-Radiant (Fever and Chill / Flint Striker), Radiant-gated Scorch+ignite add-clear (Kindling Trigger, Rays of Precision), orb generation that feeds the Innervation/Absolution/Lumina economy (Wished into Being), and champion utility (Revitalizing Blast, Anti-Champion Nosecone). No other artifact in the pool touches Radiant/Cure/Restoration or Well — the Stasis/Void/Strand/Arc artifacts (Slayer Baron's, NPA, Tablet, Hunter's Journal, Encrypted Data Disk, Implement) would strand the Solar verbs the whole build runs on. This is a mechanic fit, not an element-match: the build's regen engine is Ember of Benevolence, which fires every time you grant an ally Radiant/Cure/Restoration, and Queensfoil is the only artifact that manufactures extra Radiant sources for you to broadcast.

## Gameplay Loop
1) Open by dropping a Healing Rift in the fireteam's path — Boots of the Assembler immediately launches Noble Seekers to any ally not standing in it, healing them at range. 2) Apply Radiant/Restoration/Cure to an ally (via Lumina Noble Rounds, Healing Grenade, or an Ember of Torches melee) — this instantly procs Ember of Benevolence, supercharging your grenade/melee/class regen. 3) Scorch adds with Incinerator Snap / Solar weapon; defeating Scorched enemies (Ember of Searing) spawns Firesprites; collect them to trigger Ember of Mercy Restoration and refund melee. 4) Throw Touch of Flame Healing Grenades liberally — each one is a doubled, Restoration-granting team heal that re-procs Benevolence, so your rift comes back almost instantly. 5) Radiant stays near-permanent via Ember of Torches melees, Lumina's Noble Rounds, and Rays of Precision, so you and allies stay damage-buffed; Revitalizing Blast feeds healing and ability energy back to you on Wave-Frame GL / rocket final blows. 6) On DPS phases, drop Well of Radiance — the Well grants the team Radiant and healing, and Boots' Seekers heal anyone who steps out of the Well. Net effect: the fireteam is permanently healed, Cured/Restored, and Radiant — all hands-off via Seekers while you also clear adds.

## Strengths
Best-in-slot team sustain in the game: range-extended healing via Noble Seekers means allies get healed even when they leave your rift or Well, which is exactly where most fireteam deaths happen in GMs and raids. Near-infinite ability uptime through the Benevolence + Firesprite + Orb loop, so Healing Grenades and Rifts are almost always available. Lumina + Boots stacks two independent healing sources plus an ally damage buff. Queensfoil Censer adds extra Radiant/Cure and ignitions (Rays of Precision, Kindling Trigger), ability refunds plus bonus team Restoration (Heart of the Flame), and weapon-fed healing/ability energy (Revitalizing Blast), so you contribute meaningful uplift while babysitting the team. Heat Rises' new PvE accuracy debuff and Class >100 overshields add passive survivability. Extremely forgiving — it makes hard content trivially survivable for the whole group.

## Weaknesses
Low personal DPS and weak solo clear speed — this is a fireteam build; in solo content the Seeker/ally mechanics largely do nothing, so it underperforms outside group play. Heavily reliant on Lumina occupying your Kinetic slot, which costs you a strong legendary primary. Boots of the Assembler provides no neutral combat perk of its own, so your add-clear leans entirely on weapons and Solar verbs. Ability-economy nerfs in 9.7.0 (active cooldown cap dropped to 125%) mean you can't stat-stack rift regen as hard as in past seasons, so disciplined Benevolence proccing matters. Rift-centric play requires standing still in your Healing Rift to generate Seekers, which fights against the mobility some encounters demand.
